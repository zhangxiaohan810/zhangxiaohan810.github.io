About

I am a PhD student interested in the intersection of built environment, household energy consumption, urban climate, and data-driven urban analytics. My research explores how urban form, vegetation, land surface characteristics, and socio-economic conditions shape residential cooling behavior and energy demand.

My current work uses high-resolution spatial data, building-level features, smart meter data, and statistical or machine learning methods to understand energy consumption patterns in urban environments.

Research Interests

  • Built environment and household energy consumption
  • Cooling behavior and urban heat exposure
  • Urban vegetation, land cover, and residential electricity use
  • Smart meter data and time-series energy prediction
  • Urban resilience, outages, and climate adaptation
